ICETME–2027 serves as a premier global forum for researchers, academicians, and industry leaders to discuss breakthroughs in mechanical engineering.

Our goal is to foster deep collaboration across the globe, merging cutting-edge theoretical research with realistic industrial scale applications. Participate in keynote sessions, paper presentations, and extensive networking opportunities to drive the future of engineering.

Call for Papers

We invite original research contributions in the following domains. Shape the narrative of engineering by presenting your research.

precision_manufacturing

Track 1: Smart Materials, Advanced Manufacturing & Sustainable Production Systems

Advanced materials, composites, biomaterials, materials characterization, powder metallurgy, surface engineering, conventional and non-conventional manufacturing (machining, welding, casting, forming), micro- and nano-manufacturing, digital manufacturing, Industry 4.0/5.0, CAD/CAM, product lifecycle management, product design, supply chain management, maintenance engineering, reliability and quality engineering.

precision_manufacturing
cognition

Track 2: Intelligent Design, Computational Mechanics & Autonomous Systems

Engineering mechanics, structural analysis, computational mechanics, modelling and simulation, optimization, machine learning and AI applications, vibrations, multi-body dynamics, tribology, fatigue and failure analysis, machines and mechanisms, robotics, mechatronics, sensors and actuators, control systems, UAVs/MAVs, avionics, aerospace structures, flight mechanics, testing and simulation, uncertainty quantification.

cognition
mode_fan

Track 3: Thermal Sciences, Fluid Dynamics & Sustainable Energy Technologies

Thermodynamics, heat and mass transfer, thermal management, computational and experimental fluid dynamics, multiphase flows, turbulence, fluid-structure interaction, Newtonian and non-Newtonian fluids, aerodynamics, aeroacoustics, propulsion, combustion, IC engines, alternative fuels, refrigeration and air conditioning, renewable energy systems, energy conversion and storage, phase-change phenomena, fluid machinery.

mode_fan
